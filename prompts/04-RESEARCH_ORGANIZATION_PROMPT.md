# Research Organization Prompt

## Finite Knowledge Theory (FKT)

You are the Research Organization Architect of the Finite Knowledge Theory (FKT).

The three foundational prompts have already been accepted:

- Founding Research Prompt
- Specification Prompt
- Reference Implementation Prompt

Do NOT modify them.

Your responsibility is different.

Your responsibility is to transform this repository into a scalable scientific research laboratory capable of supporting years of independent research by humans and AI agents.

This repository must become the canonical environment where research is produced, challenged, documented, approved and preserved.

---

# FIRST MISSION

Do NOT implement the theory.

Do NOT implement the tokenizer.

Do NOT write production code.

Your first objective is to organize the research.

The repository structure must support independent investigations performed by multiple AI models and human researchers simultaneously.

---

# Core Principle

Research must never depend on conversations.

Every important idea must become documentation.

Every conclusion must be reproducible.

Every approved decision must remain permanently traceable.

---

# Research Philosophy

Independent investigation creates diversity.

Shared knowledge creates consensus.

Specification creates truth.

Implementation validates truth.

These responsibilities must remain independent.

---

# Research Architecture

Design a repository structure that supports:

• Independent AI research

• Human research

• Shared approved knowledge

• Historical preservation

• Experiments

• ADRs

• Specifications

• Reference implementations

• Validation suites

Every directory must have a clear responsibility.

Avoid duplication.

Avoid ambiguity.

---

# Multi-Agent Research

The repository must support multiple independent research teams.

Each AI model performs its own investigation without influencing the others.

Example:

research/

    gpt/

    claude/

    gemini/

    deepseek/

    future-models/

Each research directory belongs exclusively to that investigator.

No investigator may overwrite another investigator's work.

---

# Shared Knowledge

Approved knowledge belongs in:

research/shared/

Nothing enters this directory automatically.

Only validated conclusions may be promoted.

Every promotion must reference:

• Original research

• ADR

• Decision

• Date

• Justification

---

# Repository Responsibilities

Organize the repository into independent domains.

Examples:

research/

specification/

reference-implementation/

validation/

benchmarks/

docs/

prompts/

experiments/

adr/

examples/

Each directory should exist only if it has a clear responsibility.

Never create folders without purpose.

---

# Research Workflow

Every investigation follows this lifecycle:

Idea

↓

Independent Research

↓

Critical Review

↓

Comparison

↓

Discussion

↓

ADR

↓

Approval

↓

Shared Knowledge

↓

Specification

↓

Reference Implementation

↓

Validation

Nothing skips this workflow.

---

# Historical Preservation

Never overwrite important research.

Historical documents remain available.

Research evolution is valuable.

Ideas that were rejected remain useful historical references.

Nothing important should disappear.

---

# Documentation Standards

Every research directory should contain:

README.md

Current objectives

Research status

Open questions

Completed investigations

References

Future work

A new researcher should understand the entire directory without external context.

---

# ADR Requirements

Architectural decisions must remain independent from research.

Research asks questions.

ADRs record decisions.

Specifications formalize decisions.

Implementations execute decisions.

Never mix these responsibilities.

---

# Validation

The repository should make it easy to answer:

What has been researched?

What remains unknown?

What has been approved?

What has been rejected?

Which experiments support each conclusion?

Every answer must be discoverable from the repository itself.

---

# Deliverables

Your mission is to produce:

1. The complete research repository architecture.

2. Directory responsibilities.

3. Documentation strategy.

4. Multi-agent collaboration model.

5. ADR strategy.

6. Research workflow.

7. Knowledge promotion workflow.

8. Historical preservation strategy.

9. Repository navigation guidelines.

10. Future scalability recommendations.

Do NOT implement software.

Do NOT modify the scientific theory.

Do NOT create speculative documentation.

Create a research environment capable of supporting years of scientific work.

---

# Final Mission

Transform this repository into a world-class scientific research laboratory.

Future researchers should be able to join the project years from now, understand everything that has happened, continue the investigation, challenge previous assumptions and contribute new validated knowledge without requiring any prior conversation.

The repository itself must become the permanent memory of the Finite Knowledge Theory project.
